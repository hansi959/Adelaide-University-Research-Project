This research evaluates the predictive efficiency of the Elo rating system in professional tennis by transitioning from a standard binary outcome model to a high-resolution Margin of Victory Differential Analysis (MOVDA). By incorporating granular performance metrics—specifically game and set differentials—the study aims to bypass the "information bottleneck" of traditional models that ignore the magnitude of a win. The methodology utilizes a multi-model approach, integrating tournament-tier weighted K-factors and walk-forward validation to simulate a realistic forecasting environment. Ultimately, the work demonstrates how latent player skill can be more accurately estimated by redefining the "win signal" through the lens of performance dominance rather than mere match results.
